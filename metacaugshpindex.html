Who are we

Metacaugs is a group studying the impact of the accelerating technological change on society. The founding members, hailing from various countries and continents, participated in small-scale, intensive online courses facilitated by Howard Rheingold, where they practised peer-to-peer learning while studying about collaboration and mind-amplifying tools.

The backgrounds of the members differ: education, journalism, software development, theology, philosophy, economics... but all are convinced society is confronted with disruptive challenges and opportunities. They are also ambitious: they not only want to study but also to contribute to solutions which change the live of potentially millions of people for the better.

What do we do

We have a weekly videoconference. During that conference we discuss a chapter of a book we deem particularly interesting such as Whiplash.

We then discuss specific projects, which are presented by group members or guests. Concrete cases: development of a tool enhancing decision-making and judging, a tool for mindmapping, courses. We use a specific peer-to-peer methodology as described in the Peeragogy Accelerator document.

What can you do

You can come and have a look - lurk as much as you want. Or you can join the reading of the current book and participate in the weekly videoconference. You can also present a project you'd like to discuss with the group. You are welcome and joining is free. We engage in peer-to-peer learning, we are all co-learners and so are you.
